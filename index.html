Summary: America's Opioid Pandemic

In this project, we use machine learning models such as Multi-Linear Regression and Times Series ARIMA to predict future Opioid prescription claim amounts. The historical data gathered included included opioid claim amounts from 2006 - 2017 in the United States. The Opioid Epedemic began in the early 2000s as opioids were initially prescribed by doctors for physical pain management. As the prescription claim amount increased, research began to discovery the reason for addiction and manage usage.

Mulitple Linear Regression for state level prediction for missing year in CMS dataset

The Multiple Linear Regression model was to get a feel for the overall opioid trending for each state. This model shows basic linear trending data from one year to the next.

ARIMA Time-Series Model for national level prediction multiple years in the future

The ARIMA Time Series Model is able to predict future year trends based on historic data. This model provides insight as to what the opioid prescription amounts will look like in the next 10 years.
